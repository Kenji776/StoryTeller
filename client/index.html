📖 How to Play StoryTeller

What is this? Getting Started Taking Your Turn Dice Rolls Health & Conditions Resting Turn Timer Export & Rejoin Map & Chat Hosts & Admin Tips

🎲 What is this?

StoryTeller is a cooperative AI-powered D&D-style game. One player hosts a lobby and an AI Dungeon Master narrates the adventure in real time. Players take turns describing their actions and the DM decides what happens — for better or worse. It's loosely based on D&D 5e rules but don't expect it to follow them perfectly.

🏰 Getting Started

  • Create a Lobby — Click "Create Lobby" and share the 6-character join code with your friends. You can optionally password-protect the game with the 🔒 checkbox.
  • Join a Lobby — Enter the 6-character code and click Join. Public games also appear in the Active Adventures list.
  • Build your Character — Fill in your name, race, class, stats, abilities, and backstory in the lobby before the game starts. Click Save Sheet when done.
  • Export your Character — Hit 📤 Export to save a .stchar file to your computer. Do this before leaving! You'll need it to rejoin later.
  • Start the Game — Once all players are ready, the host clicks Start Game.

⚔️ Taking Your Turn

  • The current player's name is highlighted. Only they can act — everyone else watches.
  • Type what your character does in the action box and click Take Action. Be descriptive — the DM responds to what you write.
  • Use the 💡 Quick Actions dropdown as a shortcut for common actions (look around, persuade, attack, etc.).
  • The DM may suggest contextual actions in the DM Suggests section of that dropdown.
  • Use the 🎤 mic button to speak your action instead of typing.

🎲 Dice Rolls

  • Dice buttons (d4 through d20) are disabled by default. The DM unlocks the appropriate die when a roll is required.
  • When the DM requests a roll, the required die will glow and pulse. Click it to roll — your stat modifiers are applied automatically.
  • The result is sent to the DM to factor into the outcome.

❤️ Health & Conditions

  • Your HP bar is shown in the party panel on the right. If it hits 0, you're downed.
  • The DM can inflict conditions (Poisoned, Stunned, Blinded, etc.) that affect your abilities. Hover over a condition tag to see its effect, or click the ? button on the Party table header for the full reference.
  • Conditions are cleared on a long rest.

⛺ Resting

  • On your turn, open 💡 Quick Actions and choose ⛺ Propose Short Rest or 🛏️ Propose Long Rest.
  • A vote modal opens for the whole party. Everyone has 120 seconds to vote Yes or No. Non-voters are automatically counted as No.
  • Short Rest — Recovers ~⅓ of max HP. Takes 1–2 in-game hours.
  • Long Rest — Restores full HP and clears all conditions. Takes a full 8 in-game hours.
  • If the vote passes, the DM narrates the rest before the next turn begins.

⏱ Turn Timer

  • The host can enable a turn timer in ⚙️ Game Options. When enabled, each player has a set number of minutes to act.
  • If a player misses too many turns in a row they are skipped automatically — keeps things moving when someone goes AFK.
  • The timer resets whenever the active player takes an action.

💾 Character Export & Rejoin

  • Click 📤 Export (in the lobby or in-game) to download your character as a .stchar file. Keep it safe — it contains your full character sheet including XP, gold, and inventory.
  • If you disconnect and come back, find the game in the Active Adventures list and click it. You'll see a list of absent character slots.
  • Click Upload .stchar next to your character's name and select your file. The game cryptographically verifies the file before letting you back in.
  • Click 📥 Import in the lobby to load a previously saved character into the character builder.

🗺️ Map & 💬 Chat

  • Click 🗺 Map to open the live map. The DM updates it as the party explores — your icon moves to reflect where you are in the world.
  • The map is view-only for players. Only the host controls what appears on it.
  • Click 💬 Chat to open the side panel and talk to other players out-of-character without interrupting the story.

👢 Hosts & Admin

  • The player who creates the lobby is the host. They can kick players from the lobby screen before the game starts.
  • Hosts have access to an Admin Panel (separate browser tab) where they can adjust HP, apply or remove conditions, trigger dice rolls, and more.
  • The Admin Panel is your escape hatch for when the AI goes completely off the rails.

💡 Tips

  • The AI DM is powerful but unpredictable. If it ignores your action, try rephrasing it more specifically.
  • The DM responds much better to vivid, in-character descriptions. "I draw my sword and charge at the goblin, aiming for its throat" beats "attack".
  • Export your character regularly — especially after levelling up or finding rare items.
  • If something breaks badly, the host can use the Admin Panel to correct game state directly without bothering the AI.
